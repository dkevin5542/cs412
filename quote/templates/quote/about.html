About Sun Tzu

Sun Tzu (c. 544 – c. 496 BC) was a Chinese general, military strategist, writer, and philosopher. He is traditionally credited as the author of "The Art of War", a treatise on military strategy that has influenced both Eastern and Western thought. His works focus on tactics, strategy, and leadership, emphasizing flexibility, knowledge of oneself and the enemy, and the importance of strategy over brute force.

Sun Tzu’s wisdom has transcended the battlefield, influencing leaders in politics, business, and beyond for over 2,500 years. His quote, "The greatest victory is that which requires no battle," speaks to his belief in the power of strategic thinking and conflict resolution.
